Frenchonion soup

INGREDIENTS:
  1. 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  2. 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  3. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  4. 4 cups beef broth
  5. 2 cups chicken broth
  6. 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  7. 1 teaspoon sugar
  8. 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  9. Salt and pepper to taste
  10. Baguette slices
  11. Gruyere or Swiss cheese, grated
INSTRUCTIONS:
  1. In a large pot, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions to the pot and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are caramelized and golden brown. This usually takes about 30-40 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle the sugar over the onions and continue to cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the beef broth, chicken broth, white wine (if using), dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring the mixture to a simmer
    and let it cook for about 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. While the soup is simmering, preheat your oven's broiler.
  6. Ladle the soup into oven-safe bowls and place a few baguette slices on top of each bowl.
  7. Sprinkle the grated cheese generously over the baguette slices.
  8. Place the bowls under the broiler for a few minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Carefully remove the bowls from the oven (they will be hot!) and let them cool for a minute before serving.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious bowl of French onion soup.
