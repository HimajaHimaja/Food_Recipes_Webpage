Mutton Curry

INGREDIENTS:
  1. 500 grams mutton, cut into pieces
  2. 2 onions, finely chopped
  3. 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  4. 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  5. 2 green chilies, slit
  6. 1 cup yogurt, whisked
  7. 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked in water
  8. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  11. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  12. 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  13. 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  14. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  15. Salt to taste
  16. Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
INSTRUCTIONS:
  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. In a blender, blend the soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste.
  5. Add the cashew paste to the pan and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add the mutton pieces and cook on high heat until they are browned on all sides.
  7. Reduce the heat to medium and add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
  8. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined and the mutton is coated with the masala.
  9. Add the whisked yogurt and salt. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  10. Add a cup of water, cover the pan, and let it simmer on low heat for about 1-2 hours or until the mutton is tender.
  11. Check the seasoning and adjust salt and spices according to your taste.
  12. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan bread or rice.
