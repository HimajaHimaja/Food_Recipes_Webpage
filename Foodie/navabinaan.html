Navaabi Naan

INGREDIENTS:
  1. 2 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  3. 1 teaspoon sugar
  4. 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  5. 1/4 cup warm water
  6. 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  7. 2 tablespoons melted ghee or butter
  8. 1/4 cup milk
  9. Extra flour for rolling
  10. 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  11. 1/4 cup finely chopped cilantro
  12. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
INSTRUCTIONS:
  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar and yeast in warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture, yogurt, melted ghee or butter, and milk.
  3. Mix everything together until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into small equal-sized portions.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix together the finely chopped onions, cilantro, and cumin seeds.
  7. Take one portion of dough and flatten it using your hands or a rolling pin. Place a spoonful of the onion-cilantro mixture on one half of the flattened dough.
  8. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling, sealing the edges well.
  9. Roll out the stuffed dough into an oval or round shape, about 1/4 inch thick.
  10. Heat a skillet or tawa over medium-high heat. Place the rolled Navabi naan on the skillet and cook for about 1-2 minutes until bubbles start to form on the surface.
  11. Flip the naan and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until it puffs up and gets golden brown spots.
  12. Remove the naan from the skillet and brush it with melted ghee or butter for extra flavor.
  13. Repeat the process for the remaining dough portions.
  14. Serve the hot and flavorful Navabi naan with your favorite curries or enjoy it on its own!
